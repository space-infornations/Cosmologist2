Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and is the largest planet in our solar system. Known for its Great Red Spot and extensive system of moons, Jupiter is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.

Jupiter Stats

Feature Value
Distance from Sun 778.5 million km
Diameter 139,820 km
Mass 1.90 × 1027 kg
Surface Temperature -108 °C (average)
Length of Day 9.9 hours
Length of Year 11.86 Earth years
Number of Moons 79 (including Ganymede, the largest moon in the solar system)

Interesting Facts About Jupiter

  • Jupiter has a thick atmosphere with bands of clouds and storms, including the famous Great Red Spot, a giant storm larger than Earth.
  • It has a very strong magnetic field and emits more energy than it receives from the Sun.
  • Jupiter's winds can reach speeds of up to 360 km/h (224 mph).
  • Jupiter has a faint ring system and a large number of moons, many of which are geologically active.

Exploration of Jupiter

Various space missions, including the Galileo orbiter and the Juno spacecraft, have studied Jupiter's atmosphere, magnetic field, and moons.

The Hubble Space Telescope has also provided valuable data on Jupiter's storms and atmospheric dynamics.
