Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is known for its stunning ring system. It is a gas giant with a thick atmosphere primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, and it has numerous moons, including Titan, which is larger than the planet Mercury.

Saturn Stats

Feature Value
Distance from Sun 1.43 billion km
Diameter 116,460 km
Mass 5.68 × 1026 kg
Surface Temperature -178 °C (average)
Length of Day 10.7 hours
Length of Year 29.5 Earth years
Number of Moons 83 (including Titan, the second-largest moon in the solar system)

Interesting Facts About Saturn

  • Saturn is famous for its beautiful rings, which are made up of ice and rock particles.
  • It has a very low density; if there were a body of water large enough, Saturn would float.
  • Saturn's winds can reach speeds of up to 1,800 km/h (1,100 mph).
  • Saturn has a complex atmosphere with storms and high-speed winds, similar to Jupiter.

Exploration of Saturn

The Cassini-Huygens mission provided extensive data about Saturn, its rings, and its moons, including the discovery of water-ice geysers on Enceladus.

The Huygens probe landed on Titan, revealing a world with lakes and rivers of liquid methane and ethane.
