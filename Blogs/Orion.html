The Orion Constellation

About Orion

The Orion constellation is one of the most recognizable patterns in the night sky, named after a hunter in Greek mythology. It is visible throughout the world and is particularly prominent in the winter months in the Northern Hemisphere.

Notable Features

Betelgeuse

A red supergiant star marking Orion's right shoulder. It's one of the largest known stars visible to the naked eye.

Orion's Belt

Three bright stars in a row: Alnitak, Alnilam, and Mintaka, forming one of the most distinctive features of the night sky.

How to Find Orion

Look for three bright stars in a row (Orion's Belt) during winter evenings. The constellation resembles an hourglass shape with bright stars Betelgeuse (reddish) and Rigel (blue-white) marking the shoulders and feet.
