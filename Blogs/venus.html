Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for its bright appearance, thick clouds, and intense heat. Often called Earth's sister planet, it has a similar size and composition.

Interesting Facts About Venus

  • Venus has a thick, toxic atmosphere composed primarily of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid.
  • It has the hottest surface temperature of any planet in our solar system, even hotter than Mercury.
  • The surface pressure on Venus is 92 times that of Earth's, which is equivalent to being 900 meters underwater on Earth.
  • Venus rotates very slowly on its axis, and it rotates in the opposite direction to most other planets in the solar system.

Exploration of Venus

The Soviet Union's Venera program was the first to successfully land probes on Venus, providing valuable data and images from the planet's surface.

NASA's Magellan mission mapped the surface of Venus with radar, revealing details of its geology and topography.
